Help & Guide

How every part of STL Studio works. Jump to a topic on the left, or scroll through.

Getting started

STL Studio catalogues the 3D-model files already on your drives. It never downloads or moves anything — it reads your folders, builds a searchable index, and lets you browse, tag, preview, and plan prints.

  1. Open Settings and add the folder path(s) where your models live.
  2. Click Scan Library. The first scan can take a few minutes for a large collection.
  3. Browse the Library, filter to what you want, and start tagging and queueing prints.

Everything runs locally. Your library never leaves your machine.

The Library

The main grid. Every filter lives in the URL, so you can bookmark or share a filtered view.

  • Search by name, title, description, or character.
  • Negative tag filter — clicking a tag chip cycles include → exclude → off.
  • Sort by Name, Date added, or Creator — remembered as your default.
  • Saved presets — dial in filters, save, and re-apply with one click.

Model detail

Click a card to open the model. From here you can view and switch between preview images, toggle to the 3D viewer, edit tags inline, label each STL file, download all files as a zip, or open the Kit Builder.

Triage queue

A keyboard-driven review screen for models the scanner flagged as uncertain. Work through them quickly:

→ / Space dismiss (looks fine) S skip ← go back

About & support
